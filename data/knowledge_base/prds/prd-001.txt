TITLE: Team-Level Cost Attribution Dashboard

PERSONA:
Role: Platform Admin / Data Platform Lead
Company size: 500–5,000 employees
Context: Manages 1–3 Databricks workspaces, responsible for monthly cost reporting to engineering leadership and finance. Spends 3–5 hours per week manually assembling cost reports from billing exports, cluster tags, and job run logs.
Job-to-be-done: Understand which team, product feature, or pipeline is responsible for each dollar of Databricks spend — without writing custom ETL.

PAIN POINTS:
1. "I can tell our CFO we spent $120K last month but I cannot tell her which team spent what. That conversation always ends awkwardly." — Platform Admin, enterprise fintech (TKT-008).
2. Approximately 15% of compute runs untagged, making cost attribution incomplete. Tagging is voluntary and unenforced without custom policy automation.
3. Serverless SQL warehouse costs cannot be tagged at resource creation time, creating a permanent gap in tag-based attribution.
4. Building a compensating cost dashboard in Databricks SQL takes a senior engineer ~2 weeks and requires ongoing maintenance against schema changes in system.billing_usage.

REQUIREMENTS:
1. A first-class Cost Attribution view in the Databricks Account Console showing spend broken down by: workspace, team tag, job/pipeline, and SKU — filterable by date range.
2. The view must include unattributed spend as a separate line item with drill-down to the untagged resource IDs.
3. Tag enforcement policy: admins can mark specific tags as required; untagged cluster or job creation is blocked with a clear error: "Tag 'team' is required by workspace policy. Add it to proceed."
4. Budget threshold alerts: admins define a monthly budget per team tag; Databricks sends an email/webhook alert at 80% and 100% of budget.
5. Serverless warehouse costs are attributed via query-level tagging (session tag or query tag), with a UI for analysts to set their session tag on connection.

SUCCESS CRITERIA:
- A platform admin can produce a per-team cost breakdown for any 30-day window in under 5 minutes, with zero custom SQL or data exports required.
- Zero unattributed spend for any workspace with tag enforcement enabled.
- Budget alerts fire within 15 minutes of threshold crossing.

OPEN QUESTIONS:
- Should budget hard-stops (blocking new runs once budget is exhausted) be in v1 or v2?
- How do we handle jobs that span multiple team tags (shared infrastructure pipelines)?
