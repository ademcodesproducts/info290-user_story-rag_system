[PRD] Simplified Unity Catalog Permission Management for Non-Admins

TL;DR
Managing data access in Unity Catalog currently requires high-level workspace or metastore admin privileges, creating bottlenecks in fast-moving teams. This feature introduces "Delegated Ownership" and a "Request Access" workflow, allowing data owners to manage permissions for their own schemas/tables without filing IT tickets.

---
Problem Definition, Opportunity, and Outcome

Currently, a Data Engineer who creates a new schema in Unity Catalog often cannot grant access to an Analyst unless they are a Workspace Admin or the explicit owner of the parent Catalog. This "All-or-Nothing" admin model forces teams to wait days for Jira tickets to be resolved by central IT.

The outcome is often "security debt"—admins give too many people `ADMIN` rights just to stop the tickets, which violates the principle of least privilege. By creating a localized "Access Control List" (ACL) UI within the Catalog Explorer that non-admins can use for their own assets, we speed up the data democratization process while maintaining the audit logs required by the CISO.

Users & Use Cases

1. PRIMARY USERS: Analytics Engineers, Data Owners
   • An Analytics Engineer creates a 'Gold' schema for a new project.
   • [Pain]: Cannot grant `SELECT` to the Finance team without pinging the central Platform Admin.
   • A Lead Data Scientist wants to share a specific Volume of unstructured images with a contractor.
   • [Pain]: The UI for `GRANT` statements is intimidating for non-SQL users.

2. SECONDARY USERS (Edge Cases): Platform Admins
   • An Admin needs to maintain oversight without being a bottleneck.
   • [Pain]: 60% of their "High Priority" tickets are simple permission requests.

Opportunity
This bridges the gap between the complex SQL-based `GRANT/REVOKE` system and a modern, "Google Docs-style" sharing experience. It complements Unity Catalog's core security model but adds a usable wrapper similar to Snowflake's secondary roles or AWS RAM.

JTDBs
• When I create a table, I want to quickly add my teammates as "Editors" so we can collaborate.
• When an analyst tries to query a table they don't have access to, I want them to see a "Request Access" button that notifies me.
• When I am an owner of a schema, I want to see a list of everyone who has access in a simple UI.
• When I leave a project, I want to transfer ownership of my Unity Catalog assets to a colleague.

---
Requirements and Product Definition

M0: Localized Access UI & Ownership Transfer - Min Viable

P0: "Share" Button in Catalog Explorer
   • A visible button on Tables, Schemas, and Volumes that opens a permission modal.
   • Allows searching for users/groups via SCIM sync and assigning `SELECT`, `MODIFY`, or `OWNERSHIP`.

P0: Ownership Transfer
   • Ability for a current owner to nominate a new owner without Admin intervention.

M1: Access Request Workflow - Advanced Usability

P0: "Request Access" UI
   • If a user hits a `Permission Denied` error in a notebook, provide a button to "Request Access from Owner."

P1: Permission Templates
   • Admins can define "Project Roles" (e.g., "Analyst" = SELECT + DESCRIBE) that owners can apply in one click.

P2: Expiration-based Access
   • Ability to grant access that automatically expires after 30/60/90 days (Temporal Access).

User Experience: Chloe, an Analytics Engineer, has finished the `v_sales_forecast` table.
   • (J1) She clicks the "Permissions" tab in Catalog Explorer and sees she is the owner.
   • (J2) She types "Marketing_Analyst_Group" into the search bar and selects "Read Only," then clicks Save.
   • (J3) An analyst outside that group tries to query the table, sees a "Locked" icon, and clicks "Request Access."
   • (J4) Chloe receives a notification in Databricks and approves the 30-day temporary access immediately.

Success Metrics

Focus on Adoption and Efficiency (Time-to-Access).

Objectives:
   • Efficiency: Reduce the average time from "Data Creation" to "First Downstream Query" by 50%.
   • Adoption: 70% of non-admin data creators use the "Share" button instead of SQL `GRANT`.
   • Satisfaction: Significant decrease in "Permission" related support tickets.
