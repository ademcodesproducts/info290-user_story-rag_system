### What went well
* **Databricks Dashboards** (the new Lakeview) is much faster and more intuitive than the legacy dashboarding tool.
* Sharing insights is easier now that we can use **Unity Catalog** namespacing in our SQL queries.
* Performance for large `JOIN` queries improved significantly after we ran `ANALYZE TABLE` on our core dimensions.

### What didn't go well
* The **SQL Warehouse** "Scaling" settings are aggressive; we saw 4 clusters spin up for just 10 users because of a few heavy queries.
* There's no way to "Schedule" a Lakeview dashboard to PDF/Email yet, which is a major blocker for executive reporting.
* **Visualizations** in notebooks don't always match the formatting in the SQL editor, causing confusion for analysts.
* The **Metadata** tab in the Catalog Explorer is missing descriptions for 40% of our production columns.

### Action items
* Run a "Documentation Hackathon" to populate Unity Catalog descriptions (Susan R., Aug 20).
* Optimize the SQL Warehouse scaling policy to "Cost Optimized" instead of "Reliability" (Mark P., Aug 25).
* Beta test the new "Export to PDF" feature for Dashboards as soon as it's available (Mark P., Sept 1).
