PAIN POINTS RAISED:

1. Yuki: "Hex released a notebook feature this week where you can see your teammate's cursor in real time. I showed it to Nadia. We both got a little sad. We've been asking Databricks for collaborative notebooks for two years. The gap is getting wider, not narrower."

2. Nadia: "I've been testing Databricks serverless compute for interactive notebooks. No cluster startup time is genuinely life-changing — I open a notebook and it just runs. But the environment management is worse than regular clusters. I can't install packages that require system-level dependencies, and the package set resets every session. Still faster overall but the package limitation will block some of our work."

3. Isabel: "We had an Auto Loader stream silently stall this sprint. No error, no alert, just... no new data processed for 6 hours. We found out because a downstream analyst noticed the data was stale. Our monitoring script caught it eventually but 6 hours of stale data in our pipeline is unacceptable. We need built-in stream health alerting."

DECISIONS:
- Isabel to prioritize implementing the stream health SLA monitoring script for all 12 of our Auto Loader streams before end of sprint. Tomás approved 3 days of engineering time.
- Nadia to document which packages are incompatible with serverless compute and share with the team — helps others avoid hitting the same wall.

OPEN QUESTION:
Is the serverless compute package limitation (no system-level dependencies) a permanent architectural constraint or something Databricks plans to address? Ravi to ask the CSM.
