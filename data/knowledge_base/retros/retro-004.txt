### What went well
* **System Tables** (Beta) are finally giving us a clear view of who is accessing what data via `access_events`.
* External Locations in **Unity Catalog** have replaced our messy mount points (`/mnt/`), making the storage layer much more secure.
* SCIM provisioning is now fully automated via Azure AD.

### What didn't go well
* **Credential Passthrough** is incompatible with Unity Catalog, which broke several legacy legacy workflows that relied on user-identity-based S3 access.
* Managing **Row-Level Security** (RLS) filters in UC is becoming a maintenance nightmare as we scale to 500+ users.
* The **Audit Logs** arrive in the storage bucket with a 24-hour delay, which is too slow for real-time security incident response.
* We found several "Shadow IT" workspaces that were created outside of our central **Account Console** governance.

### Action items
* Draft a migration plan for legacy "Mount Point" code to UC "Volumes" (Linda E., Apr 30).
* Evaluate "Dynamic Views" as a more scalable alternative to RLS filters (Oscar P., May 5).
* Configure the "Workspace Bindings" feature to restrict catalog visibility (Maya D., May 10).
