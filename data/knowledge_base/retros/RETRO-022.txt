PAIN POINTS RAISED:

1. Rohit: "We finally did the gold table logic update this sprint — the one we'd been putting off for 3 sprints because of the full refresh. 8 hours of pipeline downtime, 4 downstream teams blocked, 2 analysts who came to me asking why their dashboards were empty. A selective refresh feature would have made this a 30-minute operation with zero consumer impact."

2. Lily: "Cluster costs for our DLT pipelines spiked this sprint — we went from $8,000 to $14,000 month-over-month. I traced it to a configuration change that accidentally disabled autoscaling on one of our larger pipelines. The pipeline ran at max cluster size 24/7 for two weeks. There was no alert for 'cluster running at max size continuously' — we only found it in the weekly cost review."

3. Isabel: "The DLT pipeline monitoring is still manual. I check the event log every morning. If something went wrong overnight, I find out when I check it. We need push notifications for pipeline health, not pull."

DECISIONS:
- Lily to add a weekly automated check: query system.billing_usage for any DLT pipeline that ran at max cluster size for more than 48 hours straight, and send a Slack alert. Basic cost anomaly detection.
- Tomás to escalate the selective refresh request to the CSM with a concrete business impact: 3 sprints of deferred work + 8 hours of planned downtime. Should be on the product roadmap.

OPEN QUESTION:
Should we implement a more aggressive DLT pipeline split to reduce the refresh blast radius? Rohit to model whether splitting into 5 pipelines vs. current 2 would meaningfully reduce the risk.
