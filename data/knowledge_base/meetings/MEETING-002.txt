USER PAIN POINTS RAISED:

Andile: "The Model Registry has stages but no process. I can promote a model to production right now with one API call — no approval, no reviewer, no audit record. I'm a FINRA-registered firm. My compliance team is asking me how I demonstrate model oversight and I'm telling them 'we have a Slack message.' That's not going to pass an audit."

Carlos: "Experiment management is a mess. I have 847 experiments in our workspace. There's no garbage collection. There's no retention policy. I estimate we're paying $2,000 a month in artifact storage for experiments nobody will ever look at again. The API has a delete method but there's no bulk delete and the rate limits make cleanup scripts take days to run."

Mei-Ling: "Cold start on model endpoints is still a blocker. My least-active models take 45 seconds to respond from cold. My SLA is 5 seconds. The only options are always-on (too expensive for 12 low-traffic models) or accept the cold start (SLA violation). I need a third option."

DECISIONS:
- Model promotion approval workflows to be scoped for Q3 — Ananya to draft PRD with input from Andile's compliance requirements.
- Experiment retention policies added to MLflow backlog — P1.
- Multi-model endpoints: Daniel to evaluate technical feasibility and bring options back to CAB in 6 weeks.

OPEN QUESTION:
Are compliance requirements (FINRA, HIPAA) driving enough demand to justify a dedicated "Regulated Industries" feature track for ML governance? Daniel to survey CAB members and report back.
